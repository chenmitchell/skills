# International & Fine Dining — Austin

## Fine Dining Scene

Austin's fine dining has matured significantly. What was once a casual-only town now has destination restaurants.

### Tier 1: Destination Restaurants

#### Uchi
- **Cuisine**: Japanese, sushi, omakase
- **Chef**: Tyson Cole (James Beard winner)
- **Must order**: Hama Chili, omakase
- **Price**: $$$$
- **Reservations**: Essential, book 2+ weeks ahead
- **Location**: South Lamar
- **Why go**: Best sushi in Texas, consistently excellent

#### Emmer & Rye
- **Cuisine**: Modern American, grain-focused
- **Chef**: Kevin Fink
- **Must order**: Pasta, dim sum cart items
- **Price**: $$$
- **Reservations**: Recommended
- **Location**: Rainey Street
- **Why go**: Innovative, local sourcing

#### Barley Swine
- **Cuisine**: Modern American, seasonal
- **Chef**: Bryce Gilmore
- **Must order**: Tasting menu
- **Price**: $$$
- **Reservations**: Recommended
- **Location**: Burnet Road
- **Why go**: Seasonal, creative, less pretentious

#### Odd Duck
- **Cuisine**: Modern American, farm-to-table
- **Must order**: Seasonal small plates, pig face
- **Price**: $$$
- **Reservations**: Recommended
- **Location**: South Lamar
- **Why go**: Austin farm-to-table icon

### Tier 2: Excellent Upscale

- **Launderette** — Modern American, great brunch
- **Uchiko** — Uchi's North Austin sibling
- **Dai Due** — Texas game and wild foods
- **Justine's** — French bistro, East Austin
- **Intero** — Italian, pasta focus

## Reservation Tips

### Need Reservations
- Uchi / Uchiko (book 2 weeks+)
- Emmer & Rye
- Justine's (especially weekends)
- Any weekend dinner at $$$+ places

### Apps
- **Resy** — Most common
- **OpenTable** — Some spots
- **Direct** — Call or website

### Walk-In Strategy
- Bar seating often available
- Early (5:30pm) or late (9pm+)
- Weeknights easier
- Ask to be added to cancellation list
