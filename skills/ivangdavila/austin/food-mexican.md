# Tex-Mex & Mexican Food — Austin

## Understanding the Difference

### Tex-Mex
- **Origin**: Texas-Mexican fusion, developed in Texas
- **Characteristics**: Yellow cheese, cumin-heavy, larger portions
- **Staples**: Queso, fajitas, crispy tacos, combo plates
- **Examples**: Matt's El Rancho, Chuy's, Trudy's

### Interior Mexican
- **Origin**: Various Mexican regions
- **Characteristics**: Regional specificity, white cheese, complex moles
- **Staples**: Street tacos, tamales, authentic salsas
- **Examples**: Suerte, El Naranjo, Fonda San Miguel

### Breakfast Tacos (Its Own Category)
- **Origin**: Austin/San Antonio specialty
- **What**: Flour tortilla, eggs, protein, salsa
- **Ritual**: Daily breakfast for many Austinites
- **Examples**: Veracruz, Tacodeli, Joe's Bakery

## Breakfast Tacos: The Definitive Guide

### Tier 1: Essential

#### Veracruz All Natural
- **Best for**: Overall quality, cult following
- **Must order**: Migas taco, green salsa
- **Locations**: Multiple (East Austin original)
- **Wait**: Moderate, worth it
- **Price**: $4-6 per taco

#### Tacodeli
- **Best for**: Consistency, innovation
- **Must order**: Otto (refried beans, bacon, egg), Cowboy
- **Locations**: Many across Austin
- **Wait**: Usually manageable
- **Price**: $4-5 per taco

#### Joe's Bakery
- **Best for**: Old Austin authenticity
- **Must order**: Carne guisada, barbacoa
- **Location**: East Austin
- **Vibe**: No frills, real deal
- **Price**: $3-5 per taco

### Tier 2: Excellent

- **Torchy's Tacos** — Chain now, still good, creative options
- **Juan in a Million** — Famous Don Juan taco challenge
- **Cisco's** — Historic, Lyndon Johnson used to eat here
- **Tamale House East** — Classic atmosphere

### The Taco Order
- **Migas** — Eggs with tortilla strips (most Austin)
- **Barbacoa** — Slow-cooked beef cheek
- **Carne guisada** — Beef stew
- **Chorizo & egg** — Spicy sausage
- **Bean & cheese** — Simple, perfect
- **Potato & egg** — Budget classic

### Salsa Philosophy
- **Green (verde)** — Tomatillo-based, tangy, most popular
- **Red (roja)** — Tomato-based, earthier
- **Always ask for both** — Options are good
- **Veracruz green salsa** — Legendary, worth special trip

## Tex-Mex Institutions

### Matt's El Rancho
- **Status**: Austin institution since 1952
- **Best for**: Classic Tex-Mex experience
- **Must order**: Bob Armstrong dip, enchiladas
- **Location**: South Lamar
- **Vibe**: Family-friendly, old school
- **Price**: $$

### Fonda San Miguel
- **Status**: Upscale interior Mexican since 1975
- **Best for**: Special occasions, Sunday brunch
- **Must order**: Brunch buffet, mole dishes

- **Vibe**: Elegant, art-filled
- **Price**: $$$

- **Price**: $$

- **Price**: $$

## Queso: A Deep Dive

### What Is Queso
- Melted cheese dip with chiles
- Tex-Mex invention (not traditional Mexican)
- Served with chips
- Addictive, dangerous

### Best Queso in Austin

1. **Torchy's** — "Democrat" (guac, queso, diablo sauce)
2. **Matt's El Rancho** — Bob Armstrong dip (taco meat, guac, sour cream)
3. **Kerbey Lane** — Classic, 24 hours
4. **Trudy's** — Good with Mexican martinis
5. **Magnolia Cafe** — Late-night essential

### Queso Etiquette
- Share with table (or don't, no judgment)
- Re-order when empty
- Pairs with beer, margaritas
- Not a meal (but could be)

## Street Tacos

### What They Are
- Small corn tortillas
- Simple toppings (onion, cilantro, salsa)
- Often doubled tortillas
- Authentic Mexican style

### Where to Find
- **Rosita's Al Pastor** — Trompo (vertical spit)
- **Cuantos Tacos** — East Austin truck
- **Taqueria Mi Trailita** — South Austin
- **Las Trancas** — Multiple trucks

### Street Taco Order
- **Al pastor** — Pork, pineapple, must-try
- **Carnitas** — Braised pork
- **Asada** — Grilled beef
- **Lengua** — Beef tongue (adventurous)
- **Tripas** — Intestine (very adventurous)

## Margaritas & Mexican Cocktails

### Mexican Martini
- **Austin invention** — Not a martini at all
- **What**: Shaken tequila, lime, olive juice, served with olives
- **Where**: Trudy's (limit 2), many spots
- **Strong**: Be warned

### Frozen vs Rocks
- **Frozen** — Slushy, sweeter, tourist move (still delicious)
- **On the rocks** — Purist choice, stronger
- **Up** — Shaken, no ice

### Best Margaritas
1. **Trudy's** — Mexican martini institution
2. **Fresa's** — Fresh ingredients
3. **Takoba** — Strong, no sugar
4. **La Condesa** — Upscale, quality tequila

### Mezcal Spots
- **Whisler's** — Extensive mezcal menu
- **El Naranjo** — Oaxacan focus
- **Las Perlas** — Mezcal bar
