## Must-See (Worth the Hype)

### Lady Bird Lake & Hike-and-Bike Trail
- **What**: 10-mile urban trail around the lake (actually dammed Colorado River)
- **Why go**: Best free activity in Austin. Kayaking, paddleboarding, running, biking
- **When**: Early morning or sunset to avoid heat
- **Cost**: Free (rentals $15-25/hour)
- **Time needed**: 1-3 hours depending on activity
- **Skip if**: You hate outdoors or it's 40C+

### South Congress (SoCo)
- **What**: Austin's iconic shopping/dining strip
- **Why go**: Best concentration of Austin weirdness, vintage shops, food trailers
- **When**: Weekend afternoons for people-watching, weekday mornings for less crowds
- **Must-do**: Jo's Coffee (I Love You So Much wall), Allen's Boots, Hey Cupcake
- **Time needed**: 2-4 hours
- **Skip if**: You dislike walking in heat or crowds

### Barton Springs Pool
- **What**: Natural spring-fed pool at 68F (20C) year-round
- **Why go**: Quintessential Austin experience, locals' favorite
- **When**: Early morning or weekdays (weekends packed)
- **Cost**: $5-9 depending on residency
- **Time needed**: 2-4 hours
- **Skip if**: You don't swim, or it's cold outside (though regulars go year-round)

### 6th Street (East 6th)
- **What**: Bar and live music district
- **Why go**: Free live music every night, quintessential Austin nightlife
- **When**: Thursday-Saturday nights after 9pm
- **Focus on**: East 6th (Dirty 6th is frat-heavy, Rainey is upscale)
- **Time needed**: 3+ hours
- **Skip if**: You don't drink or dislike crowds

### Texas State Capitol
- **What**: Larger than US Capitol (Texans are proud of this)
- **Why go**: Free tours, impressive architecture, good history
- **When**: Weekday mornings for smaller tour groups
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours
- **Skip if**: You've seen state capitols and aren't interested in Texas politics

## Worth Your Time

### Rainey Street
- **What**: Historic bungalows converted to bars/restaurants
- **Why go**: More upscale, relaxed vibe than 6th Street
- **Best for**: 25-40 age group, craft cocktails, patio drinking
- **When**: Thursday-Saturday evenings
- **Time needed**: 2-4 hours

### The Domain
- **What**: Upscale outdoor mall and mixed-use development
- **Why go**: Good shopping, dining, Apple Store, Tesla showroom
- **When**: Afternoons, especially if you need AC escape
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours

### Mount Bonnell
- **What**: Highest point in Austin (240m), 102 steps to top
- **Why go**: Best panoramic views of Hill Country and Lake Austin
- **When**: Sunrise or sunset (parking fills fast)
- **Cost**: Free
- **Time needed**: 30-60 minutes

### Blanton Museum of Art
- **What**: UT Austin's art museum, excellent Latin American collection
- **Why go**: Quality collection, Ellsworth Kelly chapel is stunning
- **When**: Weekday afternoons (free on Thursdays)
- **Cost**: $12 (free Thursdays)
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours

### LBJ Presidential Library
- **What**: Lyndon B. Johnson's presidential library on UT campus
- **Why go**: Excellent exhibits, fascinating Texas political history
- **When**: Weekday mornings
- **Cost**: Free
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours

### Zilker Botanical Garden
- **What**: 26-acre garden with Japanese garden, nature trails
- **Why go**: Peaceful escape, good for families
- **When**: Mornings to avoid heat
- **Cost**: $4-7
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours

## Tourist Traps (Lower Priority)

### Dirty 6th Street (West 6th)
- **Reality**: College bar scene, heavy drinking, fights not uncommon late night
- **Instead**: East 6th for better music and vibes, Rainey for upscale

### South by Southwest (for tourists)
- **Reality**: Overwhelming, expensive, locals avoid it
- **Instead**: Visit March outside SXSW dates, or commit fully to the festival

### Sixth Street Bat Colony
- **Reality**: The bridge viewing is cool but overhyped
- **Instead**: Go once at sunset, but manage expectations. Bats emerge Mar-Oct only

### Austin Zoo
- **Reality**: Small, not comparable to major city zoos
- **Instead**: Natural Bridge Wildlife Ranch near San Antonio is better

## Hidden Gems

### Umlauf Sculpture Garden
- **What**: Outdoor sculpture garden near Zilker
- **Why go**: Underrated, peaceful, great for photos
- **Cost**: $7

### Hope Outdoor Gallery (RIP) Successors
- **What**: Original graffiti park closed; HOPE at Carson Creek Ranch continues legacy
- **Why go**: Street art, murals, Austin's artistic side

### Deep Eddy Pool
- **What**: Oldest swimming pool in Texas (1915), spring-fed
- **Why go**: Less touristy than Barton Springs, equally refreshing
- **Cost**: $5-9

### Mayfield Park & Preserve
- **What**: Historic cottage with free-roaming peacocks
- **Why go**: Free, unusual, great for photos
- **Time needed**: 30-60 minutes

### Secret Beach
- **What**: Hidden spot on Lady Bird Lake
- **Why go**: Locals' swimming hole, feels undiscovered
- **When**: Weekday mornings (weekend crowds found it)
