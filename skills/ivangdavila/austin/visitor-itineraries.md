# Itineraries — Austin

## 1-Day Itinerary (First-Timer)

### Morning (8am-12pm)
1. **Breakfast tacos at Veracruz All Natural** (East Austin)
   - Order: Migas taco, green salsa
   - Alternative: Tacodeli or Torchy's
   
2. **Walk South Congress** (9:30-11:30am)
   - Photo at "I Love You So Much" mural (Jo's Coffee)
   - Browse vintage shops, Allen's Boots
   - Hit Lucy in Disguise or Uncommon Objects

3. **Texas State Capitol** (11:30am-12:30pm)
   - Quick self-guided tour or 30-min guided tour
   - Free, impressive, very Texan

### Afternoon (12:30pm-6pm)
4. **Lunch on South Congress**
   - Guero's Taco Bar (Tex-Mex)
   - Or Home Slice Pizza

5. **Barton Springs Pool** (2pm-5pm)
   - Bring towel, sunscreen
   - The water is 68F year-round (refreshing shock)
   - Walk to Zilker Park after

6. **Zilker Park walk** (5pm-6pm)
   - Skyline views, people-watching
   - If time: kayak on Lady Bird Lake

### Evening (7pm-late)
7. **Dinner on East 6th**
   - Franklin Barbecue (if you pre-ordered)
   - Or Loro (Asian smokehouse)
   - Or any East Side restaurant

8. **Live music on East 6th** (9pm+)
   - Check White Horse (honky tonk)
   - Mohawk (indie rock)
   - Stubb's (larger acts)
   - Most venues free before 10pm

## 3-Day Itinerary (Comprehensive)

### Day 1: Central Austin
*(Follow 1-Day Itinerary above)*

### Day 2: Outdoors & Culture

**Morning:**
- Mount Bonnell at sunrise (7am)
  - 102 steps, best views of Lake Austin
- Breakfast at Kerbey Lane Cafe (legendary pancakes)

**Midday:**
- LBJ Presidential Library (free, 2 hours)
- Blanton Museum of Art (2 hours)

**Afternoon:**
- Lady Bird Lake kayak or paddleboard (2-3 hours)
- Rent at Texas Rowing Center

**Evening:**
- Dinner on Rainey Street
  - Emmer & Rye (upscale)
  - Banger's (sausages & beer garden)
- Bar hop the bungalows

### Day 3: Food & Shopping

**Morning:**
- **BBQ pilgrimage** (choose one):
  - Franklin Barbecue (arrive 8am, eat 11am)
  - la Barbecue (shorter line, equally legendary)
  - Terry Black's (no line, very good)

**Afternoon:**
- The Domain for shopping/AC
- Or South Lamar for local shops

**Evening:**
- Hotel San Jose patio drinks (South Congress)
- Continental Club for classic Austin music
- Late night: Chili's on 45th (IYKYK — Austin joke, but it's actually iconic)

## 7-Day Itinerary (Deep Dive)

### Days 1-3
*(Follow 3-Day Itinerary above)*

### Day 4: Day Trip - Hill Country

**Full day trip (9am-7pm):**
- Drive to Fredericksburg (1.5 hours)
- German heritage, wineries, shops
- Lunch at Altdorf Biergarten
- Wine tasting at 2-3 wineries (designated driver or tour)
- Stop at Enchanted Rock State Park (hike, reservations often needed)
- Return via scenic 290

### Day 5: Day Trip - San Antonio

**Full day trip (8am-8pm):**
- The Alamo (free, 1 hour)
- River Walk (stroll, lunch)
- Pearl District (upscale dining, breweries)
- Return by 8pm

### Day 6: Local Life

**Morning:**
- East Austin coffee (Cenote, Fleet Coffee)
- Walk the murals on East Side
- Tacos at Juan in a Million (Don Juan taco challenge)

**Midday:**
- Mueller neighborhood walk
- Thinkery (if traveling with kids)
- Or vintage shopping on North Loop

**Afternoon:**
- Deep Eddy Pool (less touristy than Barton Springs)
- Or Hamilton Pool (reservations required, 45 min drive, stunning)

**Evening:**
- East Austin dinner crawl
- Dai Due (Texas game and foraging)
- Drink at Whisler's (mezcal, craft cocktails)

### Day 7: Flex Day

**Options based on interests:**

**For Music Lovers:**
- Record shopping (Waterloo Records, End of an Ear)
- Austin City Limits Studio tour
- Live show at Antone's or Moody Theater

**For Foodies:**
- Food truck crawl (South First, East 6th)

**For Nature:**
- McKinney Falls State Park
- Wild Basin Wilderness Preserve
- Or repeat favorite (Barton Springs, kayaking)

**For Relaxation:**
- Spa day (Milk + Honey)
- Hotel pool time
- Shopping at 2nd Street District

## Seasonal Adjustments

### Summer (Jun-Sep)
- Start outdoor activities before 10am
- Heavy focus on swimming (Barton Springs, pools)
- Indoor activities 12pm-5pm (museums, malls, movies)
- Evening activities when it cools slightly

### Spring (Mar-May) & Fall (Oct-Nov)
- Perfect weather for all outdoor activities
- No timing restrictions
- Best time for Hill Country drives
- SXSW (mid-March) — avoid unless attending

### Winter (Dec-Feb)
- Cedar fever warning (Dec-Feb allergies severe)
- Most activities still available
- Great time for BBQ (no heat while waiting in line)
- Trail of Lights in December
