# Romanian Cuisine in Bucharest

## Understanding Romanian Food

Romanian cuisine reflects its history: peasant cooking elevated, Balkan influences, Ottoman legacy, French touches from 19th century aristocracy. Hearty, flavorful, meat-centric.

### Core Ingredients
- **Pork**: Most common meat
- **Cabbage**: Sour, fresh, stuffed
- **Corn**: Mămăligă (polenta) everywhere
- **Sour cream**: On everything
- **Dill**: The herb of Romania
- **Sour flavors**: Ciorbă, borș

---

## Essential Dishes

### Mici (Mititei)
**The national fast food**
- Grilled minced meat rolls (pork+beef+lamb blend)
- Served with mustard, fresh bread
- Best with beer
- Found: Every terrace, BBQ spot, Obor market
- Price: 5-10 RON for 5 pieces

**Where to try**:
- **Obor Market** — Most authentic
- **Any terrace** — Summer essential
- **Berăria H** — Touristy but good

### Sarmale
**Cabbage rolls**
- Minced meat with rice in sour cabbage
- Often with sour cream and mămăligă
- Christmas dish but year-round
- Takes hours to make well

**Where to try**:
- **Lacrimi și Sfinți** — Upscale version
- **Caru' cu Bere** — Classic setting
- **La Mama** — Reliable chain

### Ciorbă
**Sour soup**
- Base: Fermented wheat bran (borș) or lemon
- Many varieties:
  - **Ciorbă de burtă** — Tripe soup (hangover cure)
  - **Ciorbă de perișoare** — Meatball soup
  - **Ciorbă de fasole** — Bean soup
  - **Ciorbă țărănească** — "Peasant" soup

**Where to try**:
- **Hanu' lui Manuc** — Traditional
- **Any traditional restaurant** — Standard starter
- **Home cooking best** — Ask a Romanian friend

### Mămăligă
**Romanian polenta**
- Side dish, base, or main
- With cheese, sour cream, egg
- Sometimes instead of bread
- Comfort food essential

### Papanași
**Dessert doughnuts**
- Fried cottage cheese dough
- With sour cream and jam
- Extremely filling
- Share one, trust me

**Where to try**:
- **Any traditional restaurant** — Universal
- **Best versions**: Lacrimi și Sfinți, Hanu' lui Manuc

## Traditional Restaurants

### Top Traditional (Upscale)

**Lacrimi și Sfinți** ★★★★★
- Modern Romanian fine dining
- Beautiful presentations
- Expensive but worth it
- Reservations essential
- €40-60/person

**Zexe** ★★★★★
- Romanian brunch specialist
- Traditional with modern twist
- Multiple locations
- Great cozonac
- €20-35/person

### Classic Traditional

**Caru' cu Bere** ★★★★☆
- Historic 1879 beer hall
- Touristy but authentic building
- Live music sometimes
- Go for experience + food
- €25-40/person

**Hanu' lui Manuc** ★★★★☆
- Historic inn from 1808
- Courtyard dining
- Full Romanian menu
- Central location
- €20-35/person

### Local Favorites

**La Mama** ★★★★☆
- Chain but good quality
- Consistent traditional food
- Multiple locations
- Reliable, affordable
- €12-20/person

**Casa Doina** ★★★★☆
- Classic Herăstrău
- Garden setting
- Traditional menu
- Locals recommend
- €20-30/person

## Regional Specialties in Bucharest

### Transylvanian
- **Sarmale în foi de viță** — Grape leaf rolls
- **Papricaș** — Paprika stew
- **Jumări** — Crispy pork bits

### Moldovan
- **Tochitură** — Pork stew with mămăligă
- **Plăcinte** — Savory pastries
- **Better sarmale** (allegedly)

### Wallachian (Bucharest Region)
- **Drob** — Easter lamb haggis
- **Ciorbă de burtă** — Bucharest claims best
- **Mici** — Local pride

### Dobrogean (Black Sea)
- **Fish dishes** — Less common inland
- **Saramură** — Salted fish
- **Midii** — Mussels

## Street Food & Quick Eats

### Covrig
- Romanian pretzel
- Street vendors everywhere
- Plain or with seeds
- 2-5 RON

### Plăcinte
- Stuffed pastries (cheese, apple, cabbage)
- Bakeries, street vendors
- 5-10 RON

### Gogoși
- Doughnuts (not papanași)
- Simple, fried, sugared
- Street food classic
- 3-5 RON

### Shawarma
- Not Romanian but beloved
- Every corner in Bucharest
- Dristor Kebap chain famous
- 20-35 RON for meal

## Markets

### Obor Market
- Largest traditional market
- Fresh produce, meat, cheese
- Best mici experience
- Go morning for freshest
- North-east Bucharest, metro accessible

### Piața Amzei
- Central, smaller
- Fresh goods
- Near Piața Romană
- More expensive, convenient

### Piața Dorobanți
- North Bucharest
- Quality produce
- Affluent area prices

## Drinks

### Beer (Bere)
**Local brands**:
- **Ursus** — Most popular
- **Ciucaș** — Mountain brand
- **Timișoreana** — Oldest
- **Silva** — Dark option

**Craft scene**: Growing, look for Ground Zero, Hop Hooligans

### Wine (Vin)
Romania = 6th largest wine producer in Europe
- **Fetească** — White, aromatic (try Neagră for red)
- **Tămâioasă** — Sweet white
- **Grasa** — Noble rot style
- **Regions**: Dealu Mare, Cotnari, Murfatlar

**Where to try**: Crama Domnească, any wine bar

### Țuică/Palincă
- Plum brandy, national spirit
- 40-60% alcohol
- Before meals traditionally
- Homemade = strongest
- Sip, don't shoot

### Coffee
- Espresso-based standard
- Turkish coffee (cafea turcească) traditional
- Always offered to guests

## Food Calendar

### Easter
- **Drob** — Lamb organs dish
- **Pască** — Sweet bread with cheese
- **Cozonac** — Sweet bread
- **Red eggs** — Dyed eggs

### Christmas
- **Sarmale** — Required
- **Cozonac** — Essential
- **Pork** — Whole pig in villages
- **Colivă** — Memorial wheat pudding

### Lent Periods
- Many restaurants offer "post" menu (vegan)
- Traditional fasting = no animal products
- Good time for vegetarians
