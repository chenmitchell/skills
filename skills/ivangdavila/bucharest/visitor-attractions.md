# Bucharest Attractions

## Must-See (Don't Miss)

### Palace of Parliament
- **What**: Second-largest administrative building in the world (after Pentagon)
- **Why visit**: Overwhelming communist megalomania, fascinating history
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours with guided tour
- **Tips**: Book online, bring ID, tours in English available
- **Cost**: ~50 RON (~€10)
- **Skip if**: You're not interested in architecture or history

### Old Town (Centru Vechi / Lipscani)
- **What**: Historic center with cobblestone streets, bars, restaurants
- **Why visit**: Vibrant atmosphere, mix of historic and party scene
- **Time needed**: Half day to full day
- **Tips**: Best in evening for atmosphere, watch for tourist trap restaurants
- **Cost**: Free to walk, budget €20-50 for drinks/food
- **Skip if**: You hate crowds or nightlife

### Village Museum (Muzeul Satului)
- **What**: Open-air museum with 300+ traditional buildings from all Romania
- **Why visit**: Best way to see Romanian rural architecture without leaving Bucharest
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours
- **Tips**: Combine with Herăstrău Park, go on weekdays for fewer crowds
- **Cost**: ~30 RON (~€6)
- **Skip if**: Not interested in folk culture

### Herăstrău Park
- **What**: Largest park in Bucharest, surrounds lake
- **Why visit**: Beautiful escape from city, boat rentals, restaurants
- **Time needed**: 2-4 hours
- **Tips**: Rent bikes or boats in summer, walk the full perimeter for exercise
- **Cost**: Free (rentals extra)
- **Skip if**: Limited mobility (it's huge)

### Revolution Square (Piața Revoluției)
- **What**: Where 1989 revolution against Ceaușescu began
- **Why visit**: Important historical site, beautiful buildings
- **Time needed**: 30 min - 1 hour
- **Tips**: See the National Art Museum, former Royal Palace
- **Cost**: Free (museum ~20 RON)
- **Skip if**: Not interested in recent history

### Romanian Athenaeum
- **What**: Stunning 19th-century concert hall
- **Why visit**: One of Europe's most beautiful concert halls
- **Time needed**: 30 min exterior, 1-2 hours if attending concert
- **Tips**: Try to catch a philharmonic concert, cheap tickets
- **Cost**: Tour ~20 RON, concerts from 30 RON
- **Skip if**: No interest in classical music or architecture

## Worth Visiting

### Calea Victoriei
- **What**: Historic main boulevard with shops, museums, architecture
- **Why visit**: Elegant street, good for walking and architecture
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours walking
- **Tips**: Walk from Piața Victoriei to Old Town
- **Cost**: Free

### National Museum of Art
- **What**: Art collection in former Royal Palace
- **Why visit**: Good European and Romanian art collections
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours
- **Tips**: Free on first Wednesday of month
- **Cost**: ~30 RON

### Cișmigiu Gardens
- **What**: Oldest public garden in Bucharest (1847)
- **Why visit**: Beautiful, central, peaceful
- **Time needed**: 1 hour
- **Tips**: Boat rentals in summer, near center
- **Cost**: Free

### Pasajul Macca-Vilacrosse
- **What**: Covered passage with yellow glass roof
- **Why visit**: Photogenic, cafes, hookah bars
- **Time needed**: 20-30 min
- **Tips**: Go for the architecture, skip overpriced cafes
- **Cost**: Free

### Stavropoleos Church
- **What**: Small 18th-century Orthodox church
- **Why visit**: Beautiful architecture, serene courtyard
- **Time needed**: 20-30 min
- **Tips**: Hidden gem in Old Town, don't miss courtyard
- **Cost**: Free

## Skip Unless Interested

### Casa Poporului tours beyond basic
- Extended tours are repetitive after first sections
- Basic tour sufficient for most visitors

### National History Museum
- Poorly maintained, confusing layout
- Go only if very interested in Romanian history

### Bucharest panoramic tours
- City is flat, no good viewpoints
- Better to explore on foot

### Most communist-era statues
- Many removed, remaining ones are underwhelming
- Better to see communist architecture instead

## Hidden Gems

### Carturesti Carusel
- **What**: Stunning bookstore in restored 19th-century building
- **Why visit**: One of world's most beautiful bookstores
- **Tips**: Free to enter, great for photos

### Therme București
- **What**: Largest thermal spa complex in Europe
- **Why visit**: Amazing modern spa, pools, saunas
- **Time needed**: Half day minimum
- **Cost**: ~120-180 RON depending on zone

### Bellu Cemetery
- **What**: Historic cemetery with famous Romanians
- **Why visit**: Beautiful sculptures, peaceful
- **Tips**: Free, near Tineretului metro

### Carol Park
- **What**: Park with mausoleum and communist monument
- **Why visit**: Less touristy than Herăstrău, interesting history
- **Tips**: The giant communist monument is fascinating

### The communist architecture walk
- Start at Casa Poporului, walk down Bulevardul Unirii
- See the megalomaniac urban planning
- Contrast with surviving old neighborhoods nearby
