## Income Needed

### Rule of Thumb
Most landlords require **3x monthly rent** in gross income.

| Monthly Rent | Needed Gross Income | Annual Salary |
|--------------|---------------------|---------------|
| $2,000 | $6,000/month | $72,000 |
| $2,500 | $7,500/month | $90,000 |
| $3,000 | $9,000/month | $108,000 |
| $3,500 | $10,500/month | $126,000 |
| $4,000 | $12,000/month | $144,000 |

### Reality Check
- **Miami salaries are lower** than NYC/SF for same jobs
- But no state income tax helps
- Many people spend more than 30% on rent here

## Hidden Costs

### Insurance (The Florida Premium)
| Insurance Type | National Avg | Florida Avg | Notes |
|----------------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Home/Condo | $1,500/yr | $4,000+/yr | Hurricane + flood |
| Renters | $200/yr | $300-500/yr | Get it anyway |
| Car | $1,500/yr | $2,500+/yr | High fraud rates |

### Condo Fees
- Typical: $400-800/month
- Luxury: $1,000-2,000/month
- Includes: Building insurance, amenities, maintenance
- **Special assessments:** Can be $10K-50K+ (new FL laws)

### Parking
- Street: $4-6/hour in popular areas
- Garage (if not included): $200-400/month
- Valet in Brickell: $300-500/month

## Ways to Save

### Housing
- Look at Edgewater instead of Brickell
- North Beach instead of South Beach
- Consider roommates (common even for professionals)
- Negotiate rent (market is softening)

### Transportation
- If possible, live where you work (avoid car costs)
- Electric vehicle = gas savings + carpool lane access
- Uber pass for frequent riders

### Food
- Publix BOGO deals are excellent
- Cuban food is cheap and filling
- Cook at home (eating out adds up fast)
- Happy hours offer real savings

### Entertainment
- Beach is free
- Many museums have free days
- Happy hour culture is strong
- Outdoor activities are cheap (parks, beaches, running)
