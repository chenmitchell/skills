# Latin American Food in Miami (Non-Cuban)

## Venezuelan

### Essential Dishes
| Dish | Description | Where |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Arepa** | Corn cake, stuffed with fillings | Everywhere |
| **Pabellón criollo** | National dish: beef, beans, rice, plantains | Venezuelan spots |
| **Cachapa** | Sweet corn pancake with cheese | Doggi's, Arepas Factory |
| **Tequeños** | Cheese sticks in dough | Appetizer everywhere |
| **Empanadas** | Fried turnovers, various fillings | All Venezuelan restaurants |

### Best Venezuelan Restaurants
| Restaurant | Area | Price | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|-------|
| **Doggi's Arepa Bar** | Multiple | $ | Best arepas, multiple locations |
| **Arepas Factory** | Doral | $ | Authentic, huge portions |
| **Moreno's Cuba** | Doral | $$ | Mix of Cuban/Venezuelan |
| **La Fresa Francesa** | Doral | $ | Breakfast, tequeños |

### Where to Find Venezuelan Food
- **Doral** — Venezuelan capital of Miami
- **Weston** — Suburb, family restaurants
- **Brickell** — Upscale options

## Peruvian

### Essential Dishes
| Dish | Description | Where |
|------|-------------|-------|
| **Ceviche** | Raw fish cured in citrus | CVI.CHE 105, La Mar |
| **Lomo saltado** | Stir-fried beef, fries, rice | Most Peruvian spots |
| **Causa** | Potato terrine with fillings | Upscale Peruvian |
| **Anticuchos** | Grilled beef heart skewers | Authentic spots |
| **Ají de gallina** | Creamy chicken, yellow chili | Traditional restaurants |
| **Pisco sour** | National cocktail | Any Peruvian restaurant |

### Best Peruvian Restaurants
| Restaurant | Area | Price | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|-------|
| **La Mar** | Brickell | $$$ | Gastón Acurio's, stunning bay views |
| **CVI.CHE 105** | Multiple | $$ | Best ceviche, reliable |
| **Pollos & Jarras** | Multiple | $ | Rotisserie chicken, casual |
| **Osaka** | South Beach | $$$ | Nikkei (Peruvian-Japanese) |
| **Baby Jane** | Miami Beach | $$$ | Modern Peruvian |

## Mexican

**Note:** Miami's Mexican food is not its strength. The Mexican population is smaller than other Latin groups.

### Best Mexican Options
| Restaurant | Area | Price | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|-------|
| **Taquiza** | Multiple | $ | Good tacos, local chain |
| **Tacology** | Brickell | $$ | Trendy tacos |
| **Coyo Taco** | Wynwood | $ | Late night, party vibe |
| **Los Félix** | Brickell | $$$ | Upscale Mexican |

### For Authentic Mexican
- Look in Homestead (agricultural workers)
- Taco trucks in industrial areas
- Don't expect California or Texas quality

## Haitian

### Essential Dishes
| Dish | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Griot** | Fried pork chunks |
| **Pikliz** | Spicy cabbage slaw |
| **Tassot** | Fried goat or beef |
| **Diri ak djon djon** | Rice with black mushrooms |
| **Bannann peze** | Twice-fried plantains |

### Best Haitian Restaurants
| Restaurant | Area | Price |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Chef Creole** | Little Haiti | $ |
| **Tap Tap** | South Beach | $$ |
| **Chez Le Bebe** | Little Haiti | $ |

### Little Haiti Area
- NE 2nd Avenue is main drag
- Emerging art scene (Little Haiti Cultural Complex)
- Gentrifying rapidly
- Try Caribbean Market for ingredients
