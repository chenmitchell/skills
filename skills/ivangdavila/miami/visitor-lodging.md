## Neighborhood Guide

### South Beach
**Best for:** First-timers, nightlife, beach walking
- Walk to beach, restaurants, bars
- Most touristy, can be loud
- Parking is expensive ($30-50/day)

### Brickell
**Best for:** Business, young professionals, foodies
- Skyline views, rooftop pools
- No beach (15 min Uber to SoBe)
- Great restaurants, less touristy

### Wynwood/Design District
**Best for:** Art lovers, Instagram, brunch culture
- Walking distance to galleries
- No beach (20 min Uber)
- Trendy but can feel empty at night

### Coral Gables
**Best for:** Families, Mediterranean charm, quiet
- Biltmore Hotel is stunning
- Need car for everything
- Beautiful but not central

### Key Biscayne
**Best for:** Families, nature lovers, privacy
- Excellent beaches, Ritz-Carlton
- Need car, bridge toll ($2)
- Very residential, quiet

## Booking Tips

1. **Book early for:** Art Basel (Dec), Ultra (Mar), Spring Break (Mar)
2. **Best deals:** Late April-May, September-October
3. **Resort fees:** Most hotels add $30-50/night â€” ask upfront
4. **Parking:** Budget $25-50/day at most hotels
5. **Airbnb:** Good in Mid-Beach, Wynwood; avoid Downtown

**Red Flags:**
- Hotels on Collins Ave above 60th St = far from action
- "Walking distance to beach" in Brickell = lie
- Cheap hotels on Ocean Drive = loud + party crowds all night
