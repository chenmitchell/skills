## What to Pack

### Winter (Dec-Feb)
- Heavy coat (down or wool)
- Warm layers, thermals
- Waterproof boots
- Hat, gloves, scarf
- Umbrella

### Spring (Mar-May)
- Light jacket, trench coat
- Layers (mornings cold, afternoons warm)
- Rain jacket, umbrella
- Comfortable walking shoes

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- Light, breathable clothing
- Shorts, t-shirts
- Sunglasses, sunscreen
- Light rain jacket (afternoon storms)

### Fall (Sep-Nov)
- Medium jacket
- Layers, sweaters
- Light scarf
- Umbrella
- Closed-toe comfortable shoes

## Weather Surprises

1. **Summer humidity** — Heat index makes 85°F feel like 100°F
2. **Subway platforms** — NOT air-conditioned; trains are
3. **Flash floods** — Heavy summer rains flood stations/streets
4. **Nor'easters** — 1-3 major winter storms per year (12"+ snow)
5. **Wind tunnels** — Skyscrapers create brutal gusts in winter
6. **Temperature swings** — 30°F changes possible in spring/fall
7. **Late snow** — Possible through early April

## Climate Tips

- **Layers always** — Buildings are overheated in winter
- **Umbrella strategy** — Compact always; $5 street umbrellas when storms hit
- **Central Park** — Often 2-5°F cooler than Midtown in summer
- **Summer subway** — Wait near entrance until train arrives
