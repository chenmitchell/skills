# Brooklyn Neighborhoods

## Williamsburg
- **Vibe:** Hipster epicenter (maturing), indie music, vintage shops, Bedford Ave.
- **Cost:** $$$$ — 1BR $3,500-4,200
- **Transit:** Good — L, G, J/M/Z, Ferry
- **Safety:** Good (7.5/10)
- **Good For:** Young professionals, creatives, nightlife
- **Food:** Smorgasburg (weekends), Peter Luger, Lilia, L'Industrie
- **Nightlife:** Brooklyn Brewery, countless bars

## DUMBO
- **Vibe:** Cobblestones, tech startups, iconic Brooklyn Bridge views. Instagram-ready.
- **Cost:** $$$$$ — 1BR $4,000+
- **Transit:** Good — F, A/C, Ferry
- **Safety:** Very safe (8.5/10)
- **Good For:** Tech workers, entrepreneurs, photographers
- **Food:** Juliana's Pizza, Time Out Market, Celestine
- **Notable:** Brooklyn Bridge Park, Manhattan Bridge views

## Brooklyn Heights
- **Vibe:** America's first suburb. Elegant brownstones, Promenade views. Old money.
- **Cost:** $$$$$ — 1BR $3,500+
- **Transit:** Excellent — 2/3, A/C, R (8 min to FiDi)
- **Safety:** Very safe (9/10)
- **Good For:** Wealthy families, quiet elegance near Manhattan
- **Notable:** Brooklyn Heights Promenade

## Park Slope
- **Vibe:** Family paradise. Brownstones, strollers everywhere, Prospect Park.
- **Cost:** $$$$ — 1BR $3,200+
- **Transit:** Good — F/G, R, 2/3
- **Safety:** Very safe (8.5/10)
- **Good For:** Families with children, suburban feel in city
- **Food:** Al di Là, Miriam, farmer's market
- **Notable:** Prospect Park, Brooklyn Museum, Botanic Garden

## Bushwick
- **Vibe:** Street art capital, warehouse parties, emerging creative scene. Still edgy.
- **Cost:** $$ — 1BR $2,200-2,800
- **Transit:** Medium — L, J/M/Z (some areas far from subway)
- **Safety:** Medium (6/10) — improving, varies by block
- **Good For:** Artists, young creatives, budget-conscious, nightlife
- **Food:** Roberta's (pizza), Bunna Cafe (Ethiopian)
- **Nightlife:** House of Yes, warehouse parties

## Bed-Stuy (Bedford-Stuyvesant)
- **Vibe:** Historic Black neighborhood, beautiful brownstones, strong community.
- **Cost:** $$ — 1BR $2,200-2,600
- **Transit:** Medium — A/C, G, J/M/Z
- **Safety:** Medium (6/10) — improving
- **Good For:** Brownstone lovers, families seeking space
- **Food:** Peaches, Saraghina
- **Notable:** Largest brownstone concentration in Brooklyn

## Crown Heights
- **Vibe:** Diverse (Caribbean, Hasidic Jewish), rapidly changing. Brooklyn Museum adjacent.
- **Cost:** $$ — 1BR ~$2,400
- **Transit:** Good — 2/3/4/5 (express!), A/C
- **Safety:** Medium (6/10) — varies by block
- **Good For:** Budget-conscious, Midtown commuters (express trains)
- **Food:** Glady's (Caribbean), Berg'n food hall

## Greenpoint
- **Vibe:** Polish heritage meets Brooklyn hip. Quieter than Williamsburg, waterfront.
- **Cost:** $$$ — 1BR $3,000-3,500
- **Transit:** Medium — G train only (notorious), Ferry helps
- **Safety:** Good (7.5/10)
- **Good For:** Young professionals wanting calm, couples
- **Food:** Peter Pan Donut, Oxomoco (Mexican)
- **Notable:** McCarren Park, waterfront parks
