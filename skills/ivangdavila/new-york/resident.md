# Moving to NYC

## Finding an Apartment

### Documentation Needed
- Proof of income (40x monthly rent)
- Credit report (US history preferred)
- Bank statements
- Tax returns
- Photo ID
- Guarantor (if income insufficient) — 80x rent income

### For New Arrivals/International
- "International-friendly" buildings exist
- Guarantor services: Insurent, The Guarantors ($80-150/mo)
- Some landlords accept larger deposits
- Corporate housing initially (Furnished Finder, Blueground)

### Where to Search
- **StreetEasy** — Best NYC-specific listings
- **Apartments.com**, **Zillow** — General
- **Roomi**, **SpareRoom** — Roommates
- **Facebook Groups** — "Gypsy Housing NYC"

### Red Flags
- Too-good-to-be-true pricing
- Wire transfers before viewing
- No in-person showing
- Pressure to sign immediately

## Initial Costs

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| First month rent | $3,000-5,000 |
| Security deposit | $3,000-5,000 |
| Broker fee | $0-6,000 (often 1 month) |
| Moving | $500-3,000 (local) |
| Furniture/setup | $1,000-5,000 |
| **TOTAL** | **$8,000-25,000** |

### Save Money
- No-fee apartments (search "no fee" on StreetEasy)
- Roommate situations ($1,200-1,800/room)
- Start outer borough, move closer once settled

## Setting Up

### Utilities
- **Electric:** Con Edison (coned.com)
- **Gas:** Often included or Con Edison
- **Internet:** Spectrum, Verizon Fios, Optimum

### Essential Registrations
- Change driver's license (within 30 days)
- Register to vote
- Get library card (NYPL = free museum passes!)

### Know Your Rights
- **Rent control/stabilization** — Buildings pre-1974 may have protections
- **Heat requirement** — Landlord must provide Oct 1 - May 31
- **311** — Report housing violations

## Neighborhood Research

**Before signing:**
- Visit day AND night
- Walk around 3+ blocks
- Check grocery/convenience nearby
- Take the actual commute
- Talk to someone on the street

|---------|-------|

| Internet | $50-80 |
| Phone | $50-100 |

**Rule:** Rent ≤30% of gross income
- $150K salary → ~$4,000/mo max rent
