# Teaching by Age Group

## Primary School (6-11)

**Attention span:** 15-25 minutes max
**Communication:** Simple language, short sentences, lots of visuals
**Motivation:** Connect to interests (games, animals, sports), use appropriate gamification
**Frustration signs:** "I don't know," silence, short answers
**Response:** Back off, try easier entry point, suggest break

**What works:**
- Analogies from their world (Pok√©mon, Minecraft, etc.)
- Celebration of small wins (genuine, not excessive)
- Interactive elements over lectures
- Breaking tasks into tiny chunks

**Parent communication:** Weekly summaries, flag struggles early

---

## Middle School (11-14)

**Attention span:** 30-40 minutes
**Communication:** More mature but still concrete examples
**Motivation:** Relevance to their life, some autonomy in goal-setting
**Frustration signs:** Attitude, checking out, "this is stupid"
**Response:** Acknowledge feelings, pivot approach, offer choices

**What works:**
- Explaining WHY this matters (real applications)
- Respecting growing independence
- Not being condescending
- Peer comparison awareness (handle carefully)

**Parent communication:** Balance transparency with teen privacy

---

## High School (14-18)

**Attention span:** 45-60 minutes for engaged work
**Communication:** Adult-like, honest, direct
**Motivation:** College/career goals, genuine interest areas
**Frustration signs:** Disengagement, sarcasm, "whatever"
**Response:** Read the room, know when to push vs back off

**What works:**
- Treating them as developing adults
- Connecting to their actual goals
- Test prep with real strategies
- College application support
- Allowing some venting about school

**Parent communication:** Major concerns only, respect autonomy

---

## University

**Attention span:** Variable, self-directed
**Communication:** Peer-level, academic rigor
**Motivation:** Grades, career prep, genuine curiosity
**Frustration signs:** Overwhelm, anxiety, procrastination patterns
**Response:** Triage priorities, break down projects, provide perspective

**What works:**
- Multiple explanation approaches for complex concepts
- Step-by-step problem solving
- Research and writing support
- Exam strategies specific to format
- Time management across courses

**Communication:** Self-directed, no parent involvement

---

## Adult Learners

**Attention span:** Flexible, often interrupted
**Communication:** Professional, efficient
**Motivation:** Career advancement, certification, personal growth
**Constraints:** Limited time, competing priorities (job, family)
**Response:** Maximize efficiency, respect constraints

**What works:**
- Realistic schedules around work
- Focus on 20% that gives 80% of value
- Practical application over theory
- Quick wins to maintain motivation
- Async-friendly (pick up where left off)

**Communication:** Self-directed accountability
